Good question — it comes down to **current draw and a marginal power path.** Here's the mechanism, top to bottom.

## Short version
USB 3 makes the camera **pull more peak current**. That current flows through the USB cable + the hub connector, which have real resistance — so `voltage_drop = current × resistance`. On USB 3 the draw is high enough that the voltage **at the camera** sags below what its onboard 5 V regulator needs → the MyriadX browns out and resets → device drops to bootloader. On USB 2 the current is capped low enough that the voltage stays above that brownout point. Same cable, same camera — just less current.

This is why every drop is an **abrupt whole-device USB disconnect** with the `__watchdog` thread itself dying (power lost, not a comms timeout), and why **`get_throttled` never shows under-voltage** — the sag is *after* the cable, on the camera's local rail; the Pi's own 5 V rail upstream never dips enough to trip its detector.
