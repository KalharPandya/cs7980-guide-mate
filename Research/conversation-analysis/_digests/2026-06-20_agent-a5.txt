# Recovering & Preventing a Wedged OAK-D-LITE on Raspberry Pi 4 (USB-level, no replug)

Practical, copy-pasteable techniques to keep robot 468's OAK-D-LITE alive and to recover it from the "camera_node alive but 0 Hz / X_LINK stall" state described in your CLAUDE.md gotcha #8, without anyone walking over to physically replug it.

## Key fact you must know first: the OAK changes its USB VID/PID between states

The OAK enumerates as a **different USB device** depending on whether it has booted firmware. This matters for every script below — you cannot hard-code one ID.

| State | VID:PID | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bootloader / unbooted (MyriadX) | `03e7:2485` | what you see right after a reset, before depthai uploads the pipeline |
| Booted app (Myriad VPU) | `03e7:f63b` | what you see while `camera_node` is streaming; **only this enumerates as USB3** |

So match on **vendor `03e7`** (Movidius/Luxonis), not a fixed PID. (Source: Luxonis USB deployment guide.)

A 0 Hz stall usually means it's stuck booted-but-wedged (`03e7:f63b` present, X_LINK dead) or it silently dropped to the bootloader (`03e7:2485`). Either way the cure is a USB-level reset followed by relaunch.
