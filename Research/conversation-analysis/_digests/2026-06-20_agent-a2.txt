# OAK-D-LITE Random USB Disconnects on Raspberry Pi 4 — Root-Cause Analysis

## TL;DR

Your signature — a device cycling `03e7:2485 (bootloader/pre-boot) ↔ 03e7:f63b (booted firmware)`, disconnecting after anywhere from <1 s to ~6 min, on **both** USB3 and USB2, **with no Pi under-voltage flag** (`vcgencmd get_throttled` stays `0x0`) and **no over-current dmesg event** — is the classic fingerprint of cause **(c): an OAK on-board 5 V brownout that does not trip the Pi's under-voltage detector**, very likely compounded by **(d): the XLink watchdog** finishing the kill once the link glitches. It is **not** (a) main-rail under-voltage (the flag would latch), and **not** (b) a host-port over-current trip (dmesg would print `over-current condition`). USB2-forcing reduces but does **not** eliminate it, exactly as you've observed, because the brownout is on the OAK's own rail, not on the USB negotiated power class. The fix is to **inject power into the camera independently** of the Pi USB port (Y-adapter + 5 V supply, powered USB3 hub, or barrel-jack power).

This matches the most directly comparable documented case: a TurtleBot/Create-3 user running depthai-ros depth on an SBC measured the OAK-D-Lite at **9.5 W avg / 9.8 W peaks**, saw the host crash **without** the under-voltage detector ever firing, and **fixed it completely by adding a separate 5.1 V 2 A supply** to the OAK adapter ([create3_docs #543](https://github.com/iRobotEducation/create3_docs/discussions/543)).

# OAK-D-LITE Camera/Depth Failure Analysis & On-Robot Test Plan — robot turtlebot468

**Platform:** Raspberry Pi 4 (Ubuntu 22.04, ROS 2 Humble) + Create 3, `depthai_ros_driver` ~2.29.0, OAK-D-LITE bus-powered off the Create 3 battery via USB-C.

**Ground-truth live evidence (treated as authoritative; research reconciled against it):**
- Device currently sits in **bootloader `03e7:2485`** on USB2 (480M); `oakd_container` process alive but device NOT booted, no dmesg activity for ~76 min → **container silently gave up**.
- dmesg history: **~20 disconnect cycles**. Boots to firmware (`f63b`) on **BOTH** USB3 SuperSpeed AND USB2 high-speed, then disconnects after **<1s, 8s, 9s, 12s, 63s, 100s, up to ~6 min**. USB2 lasts longer but is NOT immune.
- **`vcgencmd get_throttled = 0x0` for this entire boot** — no under-voltage/throttle latched despite all the cycling. No USB over-current dmesg event.
- An **earlier bench test** reported `0x50000` (under-voltage + throttle had occurred). So under-voltage happens *sometimes* but did **not** co-occur with this boot's cycling.

This last point is the crux: **the repo's "root cause is power, proven by the under-voltage flag" claim (`docs/camera.md:122`) is contradicted by a full disconnect-cycle boot with a clean `0x0` flag.** The report below reconciles this.
