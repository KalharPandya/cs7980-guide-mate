# Known/documented OAK-D / OAK-D-LITE disconnect issues on Pi 4 / ARM / low-power hosts

## Issues (symptom → cause → fix)

- **RPi4 crash / brown-out / "bricked" when OAK-D-LITE on USB3** — *Symptom:* Pi 4B locks up or crashes the moment the OAK-D-LITE is plugged into the blue USB3 port via the USB-C cable, independent of software (Roboflow / DepthAI / viewer). *Host:* Raspberry Pi 4B. *Cause:* power delivery insufficiency — the OAK draws ~1 A continuous and **peak current spikes exceed what the Pi can supply (~1.2 A per port), browning out the camera**; "max export current is 1.2A for RPI4." *Fix that worked:* plug the USB-C cable into a **USB2 port** (lower demand), use a **powered USB hub**, or externally power the OAK (Y-adapter / barrel jack). — https://forums.raspberrypi.com/viewtopic.php?t=364587 and https://docs.luxonis.com/hardware/platform/deploy/to-rpi/

- **OAK browns-out / locks up under complex pipeline on Pi (power spikes)** — *Symptom:* random lockups / OAK crash during heavy pipelines (encode + NN + stereo). *Host:* Raspberry Pi (USB-powered). *Cause:* "peak power (current) spikes from OAK can go over what the Pi can handle, causing OAK to **brown-out**," made worse by encoding/AI load and (Pro) the IR laser's extra 0.25 A. *Fix:* "**Using a powered hub can prevent this, or powering fewer other things off of the Pi over USB**"; external power via barrel jack / Y-adapter. — https://docs.luxonis.com/hardware/platform/deploy/to-rpi/ and https://docs.luxonis.com/hardware/platform/deploy/
