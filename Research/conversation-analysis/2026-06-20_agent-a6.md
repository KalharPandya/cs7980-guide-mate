## Motive

The session is a focused root-cause research task. A human operator working on the cs7980-guide-mate TurtleBot 4 project asked the agent to investigate why an OAK-D-LITE depth camera (Luxonis, Movidius MyriadX) randomly disconnects over USB on a Raspberry Pi 4 running ROS 2 Humble with depthai_ros_driver. The distinctive symptom is a device that cycles between USB product IDs 03e7:2485 (bootloader) and 03e7:f63b (booted firmware), disconnecting after anywhere from under one second to about six minutes, on both USB3 and USB2, while the Pi never latches an under-voltage flag (vcgencmd get_throttled stays 0x0) and never logs a USB over-current event (file: 2026-06-20_agent-a6.jsonl, date: 2026-06-20, turn: 1).

## Topics covered

- OAK-D / OAK-D-LITE USB disconnect failure modes on a Pi 4 and how to tell them apart from on-device evidence (file: 2026-06-20_agent-a6.jsonl, date: 2026-06-20, turn: 1).
- The 03e7:2485 (bootloader) vs 03e7:f63b (booted firmware) product-ID cycle and what causes a booted device to fall back to the bootloader (file: 2026-06-20_agent-a6.jsonl, date: 2026-06-20, turns: 1, 7, 13).
- XLink watchdog / X_LINK_DEVICE_NOT_FOUND comm-reset behavior (file: 2026-06-20_agent-a6.jsonl, date: 2026-06-20, turns: 8, 38, 48).
- OAK power draw numbers (measured 9.5 W average / 9.8 W peaks on an SBC) and the separate-5V-supply fix (file: 2026-06-20_agent-a6.jsonl, date: 2026-06-20, turn: 35).
- Raspberry Pi 4 USB per-port current limits and what an over-current event looks like in dmesg (file: 2026-06-20_agent-a6.jsonl, date: 2026-06-20, turns: 40, 49, 52).
- The "no under-voltage flag but still disconnecting" signature and what it points to (file: 2026-06-20_agent-a6.jsonl, date: 2026-06-20, turns: 1, 18, 53).
- Official power fixes (Luxonis Y-adapter, powered USB3 hub, separate 5V) and whether USB2-forcing alone fully fixes the problem (file: 2026-06-20_agent-a6.jsonl, date: 2026-06-20, turns: 9, 17, 32).
